# KoR Surfaces — Operational Realizations

## Definition

Surfaces are operational realizations constrained by the KoR canon.

A surface is not the canon itself.

A surface exposes, traverses, visualizes, operationalizes, or interacts with canonical structures without redefining them.

Surfaces remain structurally subordinate to canonical invariants.

---

# Surface Properties

A valid surface may:

- visualize structures
- execute constrained operations
- expose traversal states
- coordinate interaction
- preserve continuity
- manage symbolic transitions
- audit admissibility
- orchestrate operational systems

A surface may not:

- redefine canonical invariants
- override admissibility
- bypass refusal conditions
- erase provenance continuity
- substitute itself for the canon
- claim interpretive authority over structural invariants

---

# Canonical Constraint Rule

All surfaces remain constrained by:

- SST
- admissibility
- coherence
- refusal
- provenance
- non-closure
- structural memory

No surface possesses canonical authority.

Operational convenience does not override structural admissibility.

---

# Surface Independence Principle

Surfaces may:

- evolve
- fork
- disappear
- be replaced
- be reimplemented
- diverge operationally

without invalidating the canon itself.

The canon remains structurally prior to all surfaces.

# Surface Validity Conditions

A surface remains structurally valid only insofar as it preserves:

- admissibility
- provenance continuity
- local coherence
- refusal conditions
- auditability
- traceability

Violation of canonical invariants invalidates the surface regardless of operational performance.

---

# Surface Failure Principle

Surface collapse does not imply canonical collapse.

Operational instability may invalidate a surface while leaving canonical structures intact.

Canonical continuity remains structurally independent from surface persistence.

---

# Anti-Substitution Principle

No surface may become synonymous with KoR itself.

Interfaces, runtimes, visual systems, agents, or operational frameworks do not constitute the canon.

KoR remains structurally prior to all realizations.

---

# Surface Mutation Rule

Surface evolution remains permissible provided that:

- provenance continuity remains observable
- admissibility constraints remain preserved
- refusal conditions remain enforceable
- structural auditability remains possible

Untraceable operational mutation invalidates canonical alignment.

---

# Structural Priority Rule

If conflict emerges between:

- usability and admissibility
- execution and refusal
- optimization and coherence
- automation and provenance
- operational scale and auditability

canonical invariants retain structural priority.
