# KoR Stack — Canonical Layer Architecture

## Definition

KoR Stack defines the layered architecture through which coherence is preserved across symbolic, operational, memory, and agentic systems.

Each layer depends on the admissibility constraints of the layers below it.

No upper layer may redefine a lower layer.

---

# Layer 0 — SST

Structural Stability Theory.

Defines the conditions under which a structure remains descriptively tenable under constraint.

SST establishes the foundational field prior to interpretation, optimization, or ontology.

---

# Layer 1 — Algebra of Regimes

Defines:

- constraints
- admissibility
- refusal
- non-closure
- local coherence

This layer formalizes how structures remain viable across incompatible or shifting conditions.

---

# Layer 2 — Ridge Geometry

Defines coherence corridors and admissible transitions.

Ridges represent local structural stability zones through which systems may transform without violating invariants.

Surfaces are operational projections, not the canon itself.

---

# Layer 3 — Symbolic Diffusion

Describes propagation, deformation, drift, symbolic memory, and coherence persistence.

Symbolic systems evolve under pressure, constraint, and observer-dependent deformation.

---

# Layer 4 — Runtime Systems

Operational execution layer.

Handles:

- event structures
- transitions
- orchestration
- continuity management
- operational coherence

---

# Layer 5 — Audit Systems

Verification and admissibility layer.

Handles:

- traversal verification
- provenance
- invariant checks
- structural alignment
- traceability

Audit does not assign truth or authority.

It validates admissible traversal.

---

# Layer 6 — Memory Systems

Preserves long-term structural continuity.

Memory is treated as compressed persistence of coherent relations rather than accumulation of data.

Includes:

- TraceLock
- lineage systems
- event continuity
- structural persistence

---

# Layer 7 — Agentic Systems

Constraint-preserving human-machine orchestration.

Agency is admissible only insofar as coherence remains preserved under transformation.

Refusal remains structurally prior to unsafe execution.

---

# Canonical Dependency Rule

```text
SST
→ Algebra of Regimes
→ Ridge Geometry
→ Symbolic Diffusion
→ Runtime Systems
→ Audit Systems
→ Memory Systems
→ Agentic Systems
```

No layer may override the admissibility constraints of lower layers.

---

# Canon vs Surfaces

The stack defines the canonical structural architecture.

Surfaces are local operational realizations constrained by the stack but not constitutive of it.

Surfaces may evolve, fork, disappear, or be replaced without altering the canon.
