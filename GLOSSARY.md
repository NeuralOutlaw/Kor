# KoR Glossary — Canonical Terms

## Definition

This glossary defines the canonical operational vocabulary of KoR.

Definitions remain structurally constrained by the canon.

Terms may not be reinterpreted in contradiction with canonical invariants.

---

# Admissibility

Condition under which a structure may persist through transformation without violating coherence constraints.

Admissibility precedes execution, optimization, automation, and agency.

---

# Coherence

Local structural viability under constraint.

Coherence does not imply global consistency or total closure.

---

# Refusal

Structurally valid non-execution preserving coherence when admissible traversal cannot be guaranteed.

Refusal is not failure.

---

# Refusal-First

Structural doctrine stating that refusal retains priority over unsafe execution when admissibility conditions remain unresolved.

---

# Non-Closure

Structural impossibility of complete internalization of all coherence conditions within a system.

Non-closure is a persistence condition rather than a defect.

---

# Provenance

Observable lineage continuity across transformation, mutation, traversal, or operational transition.

Provenance precedes authority.

---

# Structural Memory

Compressed continuity of coherent relations across transformation.

Memory is not equivalent to accumulation or exhaustive storage.

---

# Symbolic Diffusion

Canonical dynamic field governing propagation, drift, deformation, and persistence of symbolic structures under constraint.

---

# Drift

Progressive deformation of structures under accumulated constraint interaction.

Drift may be constrained but never fully eliminated.

---

# Ridge

Local corridor of admissible structural coherence through transformation.

Ridges define viable traversal regions under constraint.

---

# Surface

Operational realization constrained by the canon but not constitutive of it.

Surfaces may evolve independently without redefining canonical invariants.

---

# Canon

Invariant structural layer defining admissibility conditions and coherence constraints.

The canon remains structurally prior to all operational systems and surfaces.

---

# Traceability

Ability to observe structural lineage across transitions, mutation, or operational traversal.

Traceability enables auditability and provenance continuity.

---

# Auditability

Capacity for structural traversal to remain observable and verifiable under canonical constraints.

---

# Structural Stability Theory (SST)

Foundational field defining descriptive tenability under constraint.

All canonical layers depend on SST.

---

# Constraint

Structural condition limiting admissible transformation.

Constraints define the boundaries of viable coherence.

---

# Traversal

Transformation path through admissible structural states.

Traversal remains valid only insofar as coherence and provenance remain preserved.

---

# Local Coherence

Coherence constrained to a specific regime, structure, or traversal context rather than universal consistency.

---

# Canonical Invariant

Structural condition that may not be overridden by operational surfaces or implementations.

---

# Agentic System

Operational system capable of constrained action under canonical admissibility conditions.

Agentic systems remain subordinate to refusal-first constraints.

---

# Operational Surface

Executable, visual, interactive, or runtime realization constrained by the canon.

Operational surfaces do not constitute canonical authority.

---

# ΔΩ

Canonical marker of reversible structural stability across constrained transformation.

ΔΩ does not imply permanent invariance.
